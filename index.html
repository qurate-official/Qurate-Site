The Problem

Selling to classmates is a mess.

Students have always sold things to each other — but the tools have never been built for them. Facebook groups, WhatsApp broadcasts, and Instagram DMs weren't designed for this.

No way to verify who you're dealing with

Anyone can claim to be a student. You're one bad transaction away from losing money or getting ghosted entirely.

Blurry photos, misleading descriptions

Sellers overstate condition, buyers undervalue items. Every deal is a negotiation based on guesswork and trust.

No safe way to pay

Cash-first deals go wrong. UPI transfers offer no recourse. If something's wrong with the product, you're stuck.

No central place to look

Listings are scattered across a dozen platforms. The best deals disappear before you even see them.

The Cost

It adds up faster than you think.

The second-hand market for student supplies runs into thousands of crores — yet almost all of it happens through channels with zero protections. Students pay the price.
